Safe-Pulse Fluorescence Sentry: Oil Detection

Experimental Edge AI oil detection using high-power UV-A fluorescence and "Zero Harm" safety protocols.

How the Detection System Works

OceanPulse utilizes **Safe-Pulse Fluorescence** to detect hydrocarbons in total darkness. This method provides the highest possible contrast for oil detection, ensuring reliable results even for thin surface sheens that are invisible to standard cameras.

The Night Protocol: The system triggers a high-power 365nm UV strobe to make hydrocarbons glow (fluoresce) bright white/blue against the dark water. The flash is synchronized with a high-speed camera shutter to freeze water movement and eliminate wave-blur.

"Zero Harm" Safety Architecture

Operating high-power UV equipment in public port environments requires extreme safety measures. OceanPulse features a **Triple-Lock Safety Interlock** to ensure no human or marine life is exposed to harmful radiation.

Mandatory Protocol: The UV system is physically capable of firing ONLY if the following conditions are met:
  • Thermal Check (PIR): Three PIR sensors provide 360° coverage. If any human heat signature is detected within range, the system is hard-locked.
  • Proximity Veto (Ultrasonic): A downward rangefinder confirms the target zone is clear. If a boat or person is blocking the path (< 1.5m), the flash is aborted.
  • Visible Warning Strobe: A high-intensity amber beacon flashes for 2 seconds *before* the invisible UV pulse to alert nearby personnel.
  • Hardware Hard-Stop: A physical NE555 timer circuit limits the flash duration to 500ms, preventing any possibility of continuous exposure due to software bugs.

Detection Performance

OceanPulse focuses on **high-confidence monitoring** of critical areas using concentrated light energy.

  • Precision Targeting: High-intensity UV light is concentrated into a **6 square meter** circle directly under the buoy mast (North Sector).
  • Edge Intelligence: All images are processed locally on a Raspberry Pi 5. Only detection alerts and compressed thumbnails are sent over the LoRa network.
  • Fresnel Mitigation: The optical path is angled at 35° to ensure deep UV penetration and minimal surface reflection glare.
